The Art of Typography in Web Design

How the right choice of fonts can transform a webpage from ordinary to extraordinary.

Sarah Mitchell July 19, 2024 · Updated
⏱ 5 min read

Typography is one of the most powerful tools in a web designer's toolkit. The fonts you choose don't just display text — they communicate personality, set the tone, and guide the user's eye through your content. A well-chosen typeface can make a website feel trustworthy, modern, playful, or professional.

Web-Safe Fonts vs Custom Fonts

Web-safe fonts like Arial, Georgia, and Verdana are pre-installed on virtually all operating systems. They don't require any HTTP requests to load, making them extremely fast and reliable. However, they offer limited variety and may not align with your brand identity.

Custom web fonts from services like Google Fonts or Adobe Fonts give you access to thousands of beautiful typefaces. This page uses two Google Fonts: Playfair Display for headings and Inter for body text.

"Typography is the craft of endowing human language with a durable visual form." — Robert Bringhurst

Key Typography Properties in CSS

To create readable, well-spaced text, use these essential CSS properties:

  • font-family – Specifies the typeface to use
  • font-size – Sets the size (use rem for scalability)
  • font-weight – Controls boldness (400 = regular, 700 = bold)
  • line-height – Space between lines; 1.6–1.8 is ideal for body text
  • letter-spacing – Adjusts space between characters
  • color – Ensure sufficient contrast (WCAG AA: 4.5:1 ratio)

Choosing the Right Font Pairing

A classic approach is to pair a serif font for headings with a sans-serif font for body text. This creates visual contrast while maintaining harmony. On this page, Playfair Display (serif, decorative) pairs beautifully with Inter (sans-serif, clean and modern).
